# Chapter 2: The First Week in Hell

## Summary

People throw books over the railing — popping bubble wrap satisfaction. Biscuit (homeless schizophrenic Vietnam vet) explains the Library of Babel: every possible book exists here. 410 pages, 40 lines, 80 chars, ~95 characters. The search space clicks into focus and people are stunned.

Soren runs for three weeks trying to find the end of the corridor. Never finds it. Everyone is white, American, English-speaking. Returns to friends. Jed dies from 3 quarts of vodka — resurrected next morning. Alcohol works but addiction doesn't stick ("All those cravings are controllable").

Biscuit finds "sack it" — two coherent words — and weeps. He builds elaborate numerological meaning from page/line/character numbers. Predicts they'll escape in 10 years. (Ha.)

Soren meets Betty (red-haired, from Mississippi). They become a couple for 2 years. He struggles with Mormon guilt about sex, morality without cosmic rules. "How do you know how to take a moral stand, how do you behave when it turns out there are no cosmic rules?"

## Key Prose & Imagery

> "I have to admit I found a certain strange pleasure in heaving books over the side. It was a feeling akin to popping bubble-wrap."

> "'These books are nothing but garbage,' Larisa said in a disgusted voice. 'An eternity surrounded by books, but they're all nonsense.'"

> Biscuit on finding "sack it": "it's just that ..." He broke off, then said, "When I was alive on earth ... I was homeless and chronically mentally ill. I had an old green army laundry bag that I carried everything in."

> "Adding are not possible in Hell. No one here seemed to have any more than a mild psychological attraction to things."

> On Betty: "She looked radiant, like an angel, or a Greek goddess. I just stared at her."

> "My lust seemed to have disappeared as she became a real person and not just a red-headed object with a nice face."

> On moral collapse: "Where do all the things you believed go, when all the supporting structure is found to be a myth?"

## Biscuit's Library Math

- 410 pages × 40 lines × 80 chars = 1,312,000 chars per book
- ~95 keyboard characters
- Total books: 95^1,312,000
- Every book ever written exists here, plus every possible variation
- "Most books are just a random collection of symbols"
- "There's a biography of everything and everyone in this library"

## Implementable Details

- **Throwing books over the railing**: satisfying, wasteful, human. Could be a player action.
- **Finding coherent words**: Biscuit's reaction to "sack it" — weeping, assigning meaning. Our proximity/fragment system should trigger similarly emotional events. Finding even two real words should feel momentous.
- **Numerological meaning-making**: people WILL find patterns in noise. Biscuit's elaborate numerology is simultaneously touching and delusional. NPC dialogue could reference this.
- **The run**: Soren runs 1400 miles and finds nothing different. The library is functionally infinite. Our game compresses this but the feeling should be: you will never reach the end.
- **Alcohol works, addiction doesn't**: already implemented (alcohol at kiosk, no addiction mechanic).
- **Resurrection from alcohol death**: Jed dies from vodka, returns next morning. Our death/resurrection system.
- **Homogeneity**: everyone is the same demographic. We can't implement visual diversity (text game) but NPCs could comment on the sameness.
- **Two-year relationships that end**: relationships form and dissolve. NPC disposition deterioration mirrors this.
- **Moral crisis**: losing your belief system. Morale drain is the mechanical expression of this.
